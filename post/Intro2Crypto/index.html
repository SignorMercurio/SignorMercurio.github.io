密码学笔记

Introduction to Cryptography 课程的笔记。因为是笔记，所以只记了感觉需要记的和必须做的练习，可能跳跃性比较强，也懒得组织章节。

Probability

Rules

  1. If $A\subseteq B$ Then $Pr[A]\leq Pr[B]$

$$
Pr[A] =\sum Pr_{x\in A}(x)\\
Pr[B] =\sum Pr_{y\in B}(y)\\
A\subseteq B\Rightarrow\forall x\in A,x\in B\\
\sum Pr_{x\in A}(x)\leq\sum Pr_{x\in B}(x)
$$

  1. $Pr[A\cap B]\leq min(Pr[A],Pr[B])$

$$
A\cap B\subseteq A\Rightarrow Pr[A\cap B]\leq Pr[A]\\
A\cap B\subseteq B\Rightarrow Pr[A\cap B]\leq Pr[B]
$$

  1. $max(Pr[A],Pr[B])\leq Pr[A\cup B]\leq Pr[A]+Pr[B]$

$$
Pr[A]\subseteq Pr[A\cup B]\Rightarrow Pr[A]\leq Pr[A\cup B]\\
Pr[B]\subseteq Pr[A\cup B]\Rightarrow Pr[B]\leq Pr[A\cup B]
$$
$$
\forall x\in A\cup B,x\in A || x\in B\\
\sum Pr_{x\in A\cup B}(x)\leq\sum Pr_{x\in A}(x)+\sum Pr_{x\in B}(x)
$$

  1. $Pr[A\cup B]=Pr[A]+Pr[B]-Pr[A\cap B]$

$$
A\cup B = A+B-A\cap B
$$
$$
Pr[A\cup B]\\
=\sum Pr_{x\in A\cup B}(x)\\
=\sum Pr_{x\in A+B-A\cap B}(x)\\
=\sum Pr_{x\in A}(x)+\sum Pr_{x\in B}(x)-\sum Pr_{x\in A\cap B}(x)\\
=Pr[A]+Pr[B]-Pr[A\cap B]
$$

  1. $Pr[A]-Pr[B]\leq Pr[A\backslash B]\leq Pr[A]$

$$
if\ \forall x\in B, x\notin A\Rightarrow Pr[A\backslash B]=Pr[A]\\
Pr[B]\geq 0\Rightarrow Pr[A]-Pr[B]\leq Pr[A\backslash B]
$$

$$
if\ \exists x\in B, x\in A\Rightarrow \sum Pr_{x\in A\backslash B}(x)\leq\sum Pr_{x\in A}(x)\Rightarrow Pr[A\backslash B]\leq Pr[A]\\
A-A\backslash B\subseteq B\\
\Rightarrow Pr[A-A\backslash B]\leq Pr[B]\\
\Rightarrow \sum Pr_{x\in A-A\backslash B}(x)\leq\sum Pr_{x\in B}(x)\\
\Rightarrow \sum Pr_{x\in A}(x)-\sum Pr_{x\in A\backslash B}(x)\leq\sum Pr_{x\in B}(x)
$$

Theorems

  • $Pr[A|B]=\frac{Pr[A\cap B]}{Pr[B]}$
  • Bayes: $Pr[A|B]=Pr[B|A]\frac{Pr[A]}{Pr[B]}$

Number Theory

  • $a|b\ if\ \exists x\in Z\ s.t.\ b=ax $
  • if $p$ is a prime and $p|ab$, then $p|a$ or $p|b$

Theorem

  1. For all $a,b$ there are $r,s\ s.t.\ gcd(a,b)=ra+sb$

$$
min=min({ra+sb|ra+sb>0})\\
gcd(a,b)|a,gcd(a,b)|b\Rightarrow gcd(a,b)|ra+sb\Rightarrow gcd(a,b)|min\\
$$

$$
if\ min|a\ and\ min|b\ then\ min|gcd(a,b)\\
Suppose\ min\nmid a\ or\ min\nmid b,\ let\ a=k\cdot min+t\ (0<t<min)\\
\Rightarrow t=a-k\cdot min=a-k(ra+sb)=(1-kr)a+(-ks)b\geq min\\
\Rightarrow min|a\ and\ min|b\\
\Rightarrow min|gcd(a,b)
$$

  1. if $c|ab$ and $gcd(a,c)=1$, then $c|b$

$$
gcd(a,c)=1\Rightarrow \exists r,s\ s.t.\ ra+sc=1\\
\Rightarrow rab+scb=b\\
\Rightarrow b=c(r\frac{ab}{c}+sb)\\
\Rightarrow c|b
$$

  1. if $a|N$ and $b|N$ and $gcd(a,b)=1$, then $ab|N$

$$
let\ N=ra,\ then\ b|ra\ and\ gcd(a,b)=1\Rightarrow b|r\\
let\ r=sb\Rightarrow N=sba
$$

  1. Euclidean algorithm: if $a|b$ then $gcd(a,b)=a$, else $gcd(a,b)=gcd(a, b\ mod\ a)$

$$
d|a,d|b\Rightarrow d|b-xa\\
d|a,d|b-xa\Rightarrow d|b
$$

  1. Extended Euclidean algorithm

$$
gcd(13,18)=1,\ let\ 1=13x+18y\\
z=(a,b)\ if\ z=13a+18b\\
18\ mod\ 13\Rightarrow 5=18-13=(0,1)-(1,0)=(-1,1)\\
13\ mod\ 5\Rightarrow 3=13-2\cdot 5=(1,0)-2(-1,1)=(3,-2)\\
5\ mod\ 3\Rightarrow 2=5-3=(-1,1)-(3,-2)=(-4,3)\\
3\ mod\ 2\Rightarrow 1=3-2=(3,-2)-(-4,3)=(7,-5)\\
1=7\cdot 13-5\cdot 18
$$

  1. $a$ has multiplicative inverse module $N$ if and only if $gcd(a,N)=1$

$$
Suppose\ gcd(a,N)=1\Rightarrow ra+sN=1\Rightarrow ra\equiv 1(mod\ N)\\
Suppose\ ra\equiv 1(mod\ N)\Rightarrow ra+sN=1\Rightarrow gcd(a,N)=1
$$

  1. Lagrange’s Theorem: if $H$ is a subgroup of $G$, then $|H|$ divides $|G|$

$$
\forall g\in G,h\in H,h\leftrightarrow gh\Rightarrow|H|=|gH|\\
\forall g,g’\in G,h\in H,if\ gh=g’h\Rightarrow g=g’hh^{-1}\Rightarrow gH=g’hh^{-1}H=g’H\\
\Rightarrow \forall g,g’\in G,either\ gH=g’H\ or\ gH\cap g’H=\emptyset\\
\Rightarrow G=g_1H\cup g_2H\cup …\cup g_kH\\
\Rightarrow |G|=k|H|
$$

  1. $\forall g\in G, g^{|G|}=1$

$$
let\ H={…g^{-2},g^{-1},1,g,g^2…},k=min({k|g^i=g^{i+k},i\in Z})\\
then\ g^k=1\Rightarrow H={1,g,g^2,…,g^{k-1}}\Rightarrow |H|=k \\
H\leq G\Rightarrow k|ord(G)\Rightarrow g^{ord(G)}=1
$$

  1. Fermat’s Little Theorem: if $p$ is prime, $g^p\equiv g\ mod\ p$

$$
if\ g\equiv 0\ mod\ p, obviously\ true\\
else\ g^{p-1}=g^{ord(G)}\equiv 1\ mod\ p
$$

  1. if $p$ is prime, $Z_p^*$ is cyclic
